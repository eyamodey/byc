Why You Should Build Your Capacity ...written by Eyam Odey

Men are anxious to improve their circumstances, but are unwilling to improve themselves; they therefore remain bound. — James Allen.

When you celebrate a birthday, the joy should not just be that you are older it should also be that you are BETTER. Aging is inevitable, this is saying that so long as you are alive you definitely will get older. But if you really want growth, you have got to be deliberate about it; you have got to exercise that part of you that you desire growth in. If you don't you end up stunted or extinct as the case may be.

It has been said and I quote, "Every next level of your life demands a different version of you". So if you want to remain relevant in your field of endeavour and even in life generally, as well as keep up with the times then you've got to be constantly evolving. Evolution is still happening today as in prehistoric times and the same rule applies - evolve or be extinct!

As life progresses, you are guaranteed to face a variety of circumstances, changing environments, and new roles that require you to adapt to them. This is Why You Should Build Your Capacity so as to stand a better chance of successfully navigating the murky waters of life. It is not an easy road but as Jim Rohn said, “Don’t wish it were easier, wish you were better. Don’t wish for fewer problems, wish for more skills. Don’t wish for less challenge, wish for more wisdom.”

The following are 6 of the most important benefits of having a capacity building mindset:
  1. Self-awareness: you become conscious of your strengths, and weaknesses, and the possible actions you could take.
  2. A sense of direction: with awareness comes purpose, you get a clear cut vision of where/what you want to go/be.
  3. Improved focus and effectiveness: action without direction is usually wasted. But when they are in combination increased productivity happens!
  4. More motivation: research shows you can influence your own levels of motivation. Hence, when points 1, 2 & 3 are in place, motivation naturally happens.
  5. Greater resilience: like they say, "if it doesn't kill you it makes you stronger"!
  6. More fulfilling relationships: your social network does get better, in quality that is because its size may or may not decrease depending on the number of people you'd need to weed out.

It is Jim Rohn that also said, "To solve any problem, there are three questions to ask yourself: First, what could I do? Second, what could I read? Third, who could I ask? ". We are here to help you answer these questions or at least point you in the right direction of getting answers. Thank you.
