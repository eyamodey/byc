The Need To Embrace Technology ...written by Eyam Odey

In the late 1960s, Marshall McLuhan speculated that TV and other new telecommunications technologies were transforming the world into a “global village.” Events since have shown that McLuhan’s assertion was on target. An onslaught of mass media then, and lately a new wave of digital technologies typified by the internet, seemed indeed to be shrinking the world into a single community.

Nikola Tesla had also stated in the year 1926 and I quote, "When wireless is perfectly applied the whole earth will be converted into a huge brain, which in fact it is, all things being particles of a real and rhythmic whole. We shall be able to communicate with one another instantly, irrespective of distance. Not only this, but through television and telephony we shall see and hear one another as perfectly as though we were face to face, despite intervening distances of thousands of miles; and the instruments through which we shall be able to do this will be amazingly simple compared with our present telephone. A man will be able to carry one in his vest pocket."

Isn’t it sad that most of us in this millennium, living right in the middle of the unfolding of these predictions, are oblivious of it? We seem to have not been able to harness the power of technology in our everyday living: business, job, education, hobby, family etc. Most don’t even know how to use a computer system!

What is Technology?

It is the study, development, and application of devices, machines, and techniques for manufacturing and productive processes.          Microsoft® Encarta®. The focus of this article would be to stress the “application” part of this definition and leave the others to governments and organizations with the know-how and resources to really engage in that regard.

Points To Note
  • Technology is no longer a luxury to be enjoyed by an elite few but a requirement necessary for surviving in this age.
  • Technology has always existed and is always evolving. Only those who are able to evolve with the changing technology will dominate in their niche.
WHY EMBRACE TECHNOLOGY?

There was a time when there was no cash; all trade was done by barter. At that time the quantity of goods exchanged for someone else’s goods or services was a function of the value placed on such by one or both parties. Today, money is what is mainly being exchanged for value; hence the real money is the VALUE it is being exchanged for. People pay for value; therefore to attract more money (better reward) you must increase your value. And there is no better way to increase value in any area other than the application of relevant technology.

Your value is a function of your training and/or talent, tools and time i.e.
Training / Talent + Tools + Time = REWARD.

How much you are able to apply technology to these three elements determines what reward men give to you. And this is because the world is now a “global village” by virtue of the internet which has moved us from physical to virtual transactions and interactions. Technology has transformed life from being sequential to being concurrent.
